1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is the protection of your personal information that companies store about yourself such as address, phone number and date of birth. There is a limit of what information they can hold on you and How long they can obtain it (usually 3 months) when it is no longer needed they must delete it or they can face a fine of 4% global annual turn over or 150, 000 euros (whichever is greater) this was introduced 14th of April 2016 and became enforceable on 25th May 2018. It was introduced to protect peoples information that companies contain to stop un wanted people gaining access to which may lead to Fraud or criminal activity being carried out using their information. Most companies store this information digitally on a server and they must ensure that it is secure and unavailable to hackers.
