1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright protects your work and avoids others using your own work as their own. This can be avoided by using other peoples work as your own or referencing where you got the information from. If you create your own images or content then this is identified of your own and will not be subject to copyright unless someone else uses it.

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

When it comes to designing your web page it is vital to take into account that some people may have disabilities such as being colour blind or have epilepsy so having strobing lights and banners would not be appropriate. Having a user friendly interface and strong colours will help people with the disabilities. Also avoiding small pictures and making them as large as possible for people to see and reduction of blue light.
